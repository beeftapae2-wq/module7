📖 What is Public Speaking?

Public speaking is a process of speaking in a structured, deliberate manner to inform, influence, or entertain an audience.

A speech is the term used to refer to the body of spoken expressions of information and ideas. A speech may be delivered in any of the following modes: reading from a manuscript, memorized, extemporaneous, or impromptu. The choice of mode is determined by factors such as length of preparation, complexity of the message, purpose, and occasion.

Four Modes of Delivery

📄 Reading from a Manuscript

Appropriate when the speech is long and details are complicated and essential, requiring complete accuracy. Often used when delivering a prepared speech on behalf of another speaker.

Challenge: Naturalness and eye contact may diminish because the speaker’s eyes travel between page and audience.

🧠 Memorized Speech

Excellent for short messages; also used in oratorical, declamation, and literary contests. Requires committing every word to memory.

Risk: Loss of naturalness. The worst experience is forgetting lines and failing to transition smoothly to another mode.

📝 Extemporaneous Speaking

The speaker uses an outline (short or long preparation) to guide delivery, formulating sentences while speaking. This method is common among lecturers and teachers.

Requires spontaneity, good organization, and adaptability.

⚡ Impromptu Speaking

Speaking at the spur of the moment with minimal or no preparation. Content and organization may suffer, but it brings out the most natural response.

Often used in meetings, Q&A sessions, or informal gatherings.
