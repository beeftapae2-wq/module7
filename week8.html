📖 Revision vs. Editing: What's the Difference?

🔄 Revision

"Re-vision" means seeing again. Focus on content and structure.

  • Adding or removing ideas
  • Moving paragraphs
  • Clarifying your message
  • Improving word choice

✓ Editing

Focus on correctness and polish.

  • Fixing spelling errors
  • Correcting grammar
  • Checking punctuation
  • Formatting properly

📋 Writing Process Steps

Follow these steps to create your best work. Remember, good writers revise multiple times!

1

Prewriting

Brainstorm ideas, outline, plan your purpose and audience (from Lesson 7!)

2

Drafting

Write your first version. Don't worry about perfection—just get ideas down!

3

Revising

Improve content, structure, clarity. Add, cut, move, or change parts.

4

Editing

Fix grammar, spelling, punctuation, and formatting.

5

Publishing

Share your final work with others (in your next step!)

💡 Peer Review Tip

When reviewing a classmate's work, be kind and specific. Start with what you like, then ask questions about parts that confuse you. "I really liked ____, but I was confused when ____."
